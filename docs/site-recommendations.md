# Site Recommendations by Shop Type

These are features and content that tend to work well for each type of shop. Nothing here is required — it's a menu, not a mandate. Pick what fits your business and skip what doesn't.

Your site builder (Claude) will suggest relevant items from this list during the build process. You can always say "skip that" or "add that later."

---

## Every Site

These are worth considering regardless of what you do:

**Content**
- Your name and your story (even a few sentences — customers buy trust)
- A photo of you at the bench
- Location (city/region at minimum, full address if you're a commercial shop)
- How to contact you (phone, email, form — whatever you actually check)
- Hours or availability
- Social media links

**Technical**
- Mobile-friendly layout (most of your customers will find you on their phone)
- Fast load times (Claude automatically optimizes your photos for the web)
- Contact form with a message field (powered by Netlify Forms or Formspree — set up automatically)
- **Tap-to-call button** — if you have a business phone, a prominent "Call Us" button lets mobile visitors call you directly with one tap. One of the highest-converting elements for local service businesses. Skip it if you'd rather route everyone through a form.
- **No dead ends** — every page leads somewhere next. Visitors should never have to scroll back up to the nav to find their next step.
- Basic SEO so "guitar repair near me" finds you
- Google Business Profile description (Claude generates this for you)
- An `llms.txt` file so AI assistants can accurately describe your business to potential customers

## Repair Shops

**Content**
- Service menu with pricing (or "evaluation-based" if that's your model)
- Before/after photos of your best work — these sell harder than any copy
- Turnaround times (even approximate: "most setups 3-5 days")
- Your policies: walk-in vs. appointment, drop-off process, payment methods
- Certifications and factory authorizations (Martin, Taylor, Gibson, etc.)
- Warranty/guarantee information
- What instruments you work on (and don't work on, if relevant)
- Testimonials from customers

**Features**
- Intake/contact form with fields for instrument type and issue description
- File upload on the contact form for damage photos (saves back-and-forth)
- Service category sections that are easy to scan (not a wall of text)
- FAQ section addressing common questions ("How much does a refret cost?" "Do you work on basses?")

**Nice-to-haves**
- Before/after photo slider (draggable comparison)
- "What to expect" section walking through the drop-off → evaluation → repair → pickup process
- Regional/climate callouts ("Memphis humidity is hard on instruments — here's how we help")

## Builders

**Content**
- Portfolio/gallery of completed instruments (your best 8-12, not everything you've ever made)
- Your build process (even a brief overview — customers want to understand what 300 hours looks like)
- Materials you work with (wood species, hardware sources)
- Models you offer (if you have standard models) or "fully custom" description
- Pricing or price ranges (or "contact for quote" if you prefer)
- Current waitlist/lead time
- Your background and training (apprenticeship, school, self-taught — it all matters)

**Features**
- Commission/inquiry form with fields for guitar type, preferences, and timeline
- High-quality photo gallery with enough detail to see grain, binding, inlay
- Process timeline or "how a build works" section

**Nice-to-haves**
- Horizontal-scrolling instrument showcase
- Build log or journal section (if you document your builds)
- Wood/tonewood gallery showing available stock
- Sound samples or video embeds of your instruments being played

## Hybrid Shops (Repair + Build)

Everything from both lists above, plus:

**Content**
- Clear visual separation between repair services and custom builds — these are different audiences with different needs
- A way for visitors to quickly self-sort ("Need a repair?" vs. "Want a custom instrument?")

**Features**
- Navigation that makes both sides of the business easy to find
- Separate contact/intake forms (or a single form with a "what are you looking for?" selector)

**Watch out for**
- Don't let one side of the business bury the other. If 80% of your revenue is repairs, the repair info should be easy to find — but the build portfolio can still be prominent for the right visitor.
- Don't make the site feel like two different websites stapled together. Unified brand, different sections.

## Things You Probably Don't Need

- A blog (unless you'll actually write posts — an empty blog looks worse than no blog)
- An online store (unless you sell strings/accessories/merch — use Stripe or Shopify for that, not a custom shop)
- A booking calendar widget (unless you already use Calendly/Acuity/RepairShopr — a contact form works fine for most shops)
- Animations on every element (a few signature moments are better than constant motion)
- A separate "Gallery" page if you only have 6 photos (put them on the home page)
