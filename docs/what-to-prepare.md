# What to Prepare

A simple guide to organizing your shop's materials before building your website. The AI works best when you give it good raw ingredients — just like building a guitar.

**The golden rule:** Curate, don't dump. The AI needs your best photos, not your entire camera roll.

---

## Quick Start (Minimum Viable Package)

Short on time or just want to get something up fast? Here's the bare minimum:

- **5 photos:** 1 hero shot of your best work, 1 photo of you at the bench, 1 shop/workspace shot, 2 more of completed work
- **Your services and prices** (even a quick list)
- **Your name, location, and how to contact you**

That's enough to build a solid site. You can always add more photos and content later. The AI will work with whatever you have and tell you if it needs more.

If you want to give the AI the best possible material to work with, use the full checklist below.

---

## Full Checklist

### Folder 1: Photos (`/photos`)

Select **20-30 photos total**. Quality over quantity.

**The Shop (1-3 photos)**
- A wide shot of your bench or storefront
- Your space tells customers a lot — don't skip this

**Hero Shots (3-5 photos)**
- Your absolute best work: finished instruments, extreme close-ups of perfect fret ends, highly figured wood
- Well-lit, in-focus, high-resolution
- If a photo looks bad on your phone, it'll look terrible on a website

**The "You" Shot (1 photo)**
- A well-lit photo of you working at the bench
- Customers buy trust — they need to see your face
- At the bench is better than a posed headshot

**Before and After Pairs**
- If you have photos of a repair process (broken headstock, then the finished repair), put each pair in its own sub-folder so the AI knows they go together
- These are some of the most compelling content you can have

**Your Logo**
- High-resolution PNG with a transparent background
- If you don't have a logo, that's fine — the AI will create a clean text-based one

**Format:** Standard `.jpg` or `.png` files. If your photos are in `.HEIC` format (iPhone default), don't worry — the AI will convert them automatically on Mac. On other platforms, it'll walk you through converting them.

---

## Folder 2: Your Info (`/text`)

The AI will read this to understand your business and write your website copy. You don't need to be a writer — just brain-dump the facts.

**Any text format works:** `.txt`, Word doc, or even just type it into an email to yourself.

**Your Story (3-5 sentences)**
- Why you started
- Your background (school, apprenticeship, factory experience)
- What you love fixing or building the most

**Your Services and Pricing**
- Your current service menu with prices
- If you're evaluation-based: just say "I don't list prices, every instrument is unique"
- Include turnaround times if you have them

**Your Favorite Repairs or Builds (3-4 examples)**
Write down the raw details. The AI will turn these into compelling stories.

Example: *"1972 Tele. Re-fretted with 6105 wire. Leveled radius to 9.5. Rewired with CTS pots and a 4-way switch."*

You don't need to write pretty prose — just the facts.

---

## Folder 3: Style Inspiration (`/style`)

Drop a text file with **1-3 website URLs** you think look great. They don't have to be guitar websites.

If you love the crisp, minimal look of a high-end watch company, include that link. If you like the dark, gritty look of a local coffee shop's site, include that. The AI will extract the colors, fonts, and feel from those references.

If you don't have references, that's fine — the AI will design something based on your photos and your story.

---

## That's It

Once your materials are ready — whether that's the full checklist or just the quick start minimum — open the toolkit in Claude Code and type `/luthier-interviewer` to get started. The AI will work with what you've given it and ask for anything it needs.

Don't have everything right now? Start with whatever you have. You can always add more photos and content later — the AI will tell you if it needs something specific.
