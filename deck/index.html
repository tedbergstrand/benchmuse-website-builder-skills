Website Builder

A conversational AI toolkit that builds bespoke websites for luthiers and repair shops. No templates. No monthly fees. You own the code.

BenchMuse 02 · The problem
Ask the room: how many of you have a website right now? How many of you actually like it? The pain is real: most of you are either on Squarespace paying $20/mo for a template, or you've been meaning to "get around to it" for two years.
What it is

Talk to Claude.
Get a site.

BenchMuse is five conversational AI skills that interview you about your shop, design from your photos and story, write the code, and hand you a deployable folder. It's not a template picker. It's an experienced web designer who asks good questions and then gets out of your way.

BenchMuse 03 · What it is
The key distinction: templates ask you to pick from a menu. BenchMuse asks you about your shop and designs a one-of-a-kind site from your answers. Think of it as hiring a web designer for $20 instead of $5,000.
How it works

Five steps.
One folder.

01
Interview
~20 minute conversation about your shop, services, voice, and story.
02
Design
Claude analyzes your photos and any references, picks a palette and typography, confirms direction with you.
03
Curate
Your photos get sorted, optimized, and assigned to pages with SEO alt tags.
04
Build
Claude writes the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. You review a live preview in your browser.
05
SEO
Meta tags, schema, sitemap, and a file so ChatGPT can accurately describe your shop.
BenchMuse 04 · How it works
Emphasize: you can stop and come back any time. Each step writes a handoff file, so if you run out of time on a Tuesday night, picking up Saturday morning is seamless.
What you get

Example site, generated from ~30 minutes of conversation and ~10 photos.

Most people build in two or three sessions. So real-world out-of-pocket: $20 one month, $15 a year for the domain, done. Less than what you're paying Squarespace for three months of generic.
What you own

Every line of code
is yours.

When Claude finishes, you have a folder of files on your computer. HTML, CSS, JavaScript, your photos. Deploy it to Netlify. Deploy it to anywhere else. Hand it to a developer if you outgrow it. Keep working on it yourself. Nobody can turn it off.

No subscription to your website. No editor you're locked into. No platform that raises prices next year. The files are yours the way a guitar is yours after you bought it.

BenchMuse 07 · Ownership
This is the thing Squarespace, Wix, etc. never let you have. Your site lives on their servers, in their editor, in their format. If they raise prices or shut down, so does your site. BenchMuse hands you a folder. That folder is a regular website. You own it the way you own any file on your computer.

If you already know what that means, you already know what to do.
