Your photos

iPhone is fine.

Ten or fifteen photos is plenty. Your bench. A couple of guitars you're proud of. One or two of you at work. A shot of the shop. Before-and-after of a repair you like.

Don't overthink this. If you don't have photos yet, Claude will use placeholder images and you can swap in yours later.

BenchMuse 06
Seriously — don't hire a photographer for this. Phone photos look great on a website. Natural light, decent framing, you're done. If you really have nothing, Claude can use licensed workshop photos as placeholders. Your site will still look good and you can swap in real photos any time.
When you don't like something

Tell Claude.
It fixes it.

"Make the hero smaller." "The green looks muddy — try a warmer color." "I hate this font." "Add a page for my YouTube channel." You just say it. Claude changes the site and you look at it again.

BenchMuse 07
This is the part I wish someone had told me about AI tools a year ago. You don't have to know how to describe things in technical terms. "I don't like the vibe" is a valid instruction. Claude figures out what you mean and tries something different. If you still don't like it, try again. Don't be precious about getting it right on the first pass. The whole thing is built to iterate.
Putting it on the internet

Drag a folder.
That's it.

When the site is done, you'll have a folder on your computer called website. You go to Netlify in your browser, drag that folder onto the page, and your site is live. Took me about 40 seconds the first time.

Your domain (yourshop.com) gets pointed at Netlify in two clicks. Done.

BenchMuse 08
If you can drag a file into your email, you can deploy a website on Netlify. That's not hyperbole — the interface is just a big rectangle that says "drag your folder here." Netlify also handles the HTTPS (that's the little lock icon in the URL bar) for you automatically. No fiddling.
