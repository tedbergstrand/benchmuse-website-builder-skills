BenchMuse 04
The "AI builds your website" phrase makes people picture some sci-fi process. What's actually happening: you're typing in a chat window. The AI reads your answers and writes the site. The closest comparison I can give you is having a web designer on a long text thread, except they reply instantly and they're patient about everything.

The five steps.

  1. 1 InterviewClaude asks about your shop. Around 20 minutes.
  2. 2 Design directionClaude looks at your photos, picks colors and fonts, shows you the plan.
  3. 3 PhotosClaude sorts them, shrinks them, writes descriptions.
  4. 4 BuildClaude writes the site. You see it live in your browser.
  5. 5 SEOClaude adds the pieces Google looks for.
BenchMuse 05
You do these one at a time. Each takes between five and thirty minutes. You can stop after any step and come back later. Claude remembers where you were. You start each step by typing a single word. I'll show you the exact words in a second.

iPhone is fine.

Ten or fifteen will do it. Your bench. A couple of guitars you like. One or two of you at work. A shot of the shop. No photographer needed.

If you don't have photos yet, Claude uses licensed placeholders and you swap them in later.

BenchMuse 06
Do not hire a photographer for this. Phone photos look great on the web. Good light, reasonable framing, you're set. If you have nothing, use placeholders. Swap in real photos later. Your site doesn't have to be perfect on day one.

BenchMuse 07
You don't need the technical vocabulary. "I don't like the vibe" is a valid instruction. Claude figures out what you mean and tries again. Don't be precious about getting it right the first time. The whole thing is built for iteration.

Drag a folder.

When Claude is done, your site is a folder on your computer. You open Netlify in your browser and drag the folder onto the page. Your site is live.

Took me around forty seconds the first time I tried it.

BenchMuse 08
Netlify's upload page is one big rectangle that says "drag your folder here." If you can drag a file into an email, you can deploy a website. Netlify also handles HTTPS (the lock icon) automatically. You don't need to think about it.
